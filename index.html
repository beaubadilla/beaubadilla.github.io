Pihp

There's been plenty of times when I'm with friends or family, and we cannot collectively decide on a place to eat at. What tends to happen is that one person starts randomly naming restaurants while some swipe left(say no) and others swipe right(say yes). Pihp forces everyone to be a bit more decisive through a draft and an assortment of information.

This is definitely my biggest and proudest project so far. I wanted to build this application for the last two years. After multiple attempts, failing because my skillset was not quite there yet, I finally got it out of the ground in the beginning of 2020. Although, I might have bitten off more than I could chew but I learned an incredible amount. I had become familiar with front-end tech, back-end tech, and databases individually from college courses, but not as one unit. In addition, there was much more to JavaScript(luckily, I chose it for both front-end and back-end) than I anticipated. One concept that particularly gave me a headache were promises and async/await. There were parts I naively overlooked that drastically affected the application design like 3rd party API costs, limits, and ToS(Terms of Service).
